Flagship Program · Skill Development

JEET — Job Empowerment & Employment Training

JEET is Sarthak's flagship vocational training programme for persons with disabilities aged 18–45. Participants receive sector-specific skill training, soft skills development, and direct placement support with corporate partners.

  • ✓ Trades: IT/BPO, retail, hospitality, manufacturing, beauty & wellness
  • ✓ 3–6 month certified training courses
  • ✓ 43,466+ graduates placed in employment
  • ✓ Post-placement follow-up for 12 months

Digital Platforms

Technology-powered empowerment

Sarthak has built purpose-designed digital platforms to scale its reach and match people to opportunities faster.

💼
Employment Platform

RozgarSarathi

An online employment exchange dedicated exclusively to Persons with Disabilities. Job-seekers create profiles, upload certificates, and apply directly to companies with verified inclusive hiring commitments.

  • ✓ 500+ corporate employers registered
  • ✓ Live job listings across 15 sectors
  • ✓ Resume builder & interview prep tools
📊
Capacity Building

CapSarathi

A capacity building platform for disability-sector NGOs. Organisations access training modules, compliance templates, funding databases, and peer learning forums — all in one place.

  • ✓ 600+ NGOs onboarded
  • ✓ Legal & compliance toolkits
  • ✓ Grant discovery & application support
🎓
E-Learning

GyanSarathi

A fully accessible e-learning portal offering courses in vocational skills, digital literacy, and life skills — designed from the ground up with WCAG accessibility standards for PwD learners.

  • ✓ Screen reader compatible
  • ✓ Sign-language video lessons
  • ✓ Self-paced & free to access
Our Centres

Sarthak Adarsh Kendras

Our Adarsh Kendras (Model Centres) are the physical backbone of Sarthak's work — community-based hubs that deliver the full spectrum of our services under one roof, from early intervention to employment placement.

Each Kendra is staffed with trained therapists, vocational counsellors, and placement officers. They serve as a safe, inclusive space for persons with disabilities and their families — often in cities and neighbourhoods where no other support exists.
